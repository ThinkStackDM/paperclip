# SEO Keyword Research (No Paid Tools)

The highest-signal data is free and first-party. Pick the query a page should target + find the next page to build, without Ahrefs.

## Mine demand (priority order)
1. **Google Search Console** (our best signal): Performance → Queries — the searches we already
   rank for, with impressions + position. Two goldmines: **striking distance** (pos 8–20, high
   impressions → small on-page work pushes to page 1) and **impressions with no good page** (build it).
2. **Autocomplete**: seed + a–z + modifiers (how/best/vs/for) → suggestion tree, ordered by volume.
3. **People Also Ask + Related Searches** on the SERP → the H2s/FAQ + adjacent queries.
4. Reddit/forum phrasing → real language + pain points.

## Pick the query
- **Intent match first**: does the page format match what the SERP shows (tool/listicle/how-to/definition)? Mismatch = can't rank.
- **Winnability**: scan top 10 — thin/low-authority/off-target/old = a gap; all-strong = pick a longer-tail variant.
- One primary query per page + a cluster of PAA sub-questions. Not 10 queries/page or 10 pages/query.

## On-page
Primary query in title tag (front), H1, first 100 words, URL slug. Answer PAA as headed sections + FAQ with FAQPage JSON-LD (reuse the utility-site-shipping scaffold). Be the *best* result, not keyword-stuffed. Internal-link the cluster.

## Loop
Watch GSC for the page's query at 2–4 weeks: improving → double down on the cluster; stuck on page 2 → improve depth/intent match. Programmatic SEO mechanics stay in utility-site-shipping; this is the research that picks what to build.
