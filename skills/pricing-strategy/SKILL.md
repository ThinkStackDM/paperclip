# Pricing Strategy

Price hits revenue directly with ~zero production cost to change, and the portfolio mostly picks it
by gut. Set on value + the competitive band, then test — don't default to "cheap."

## Anchor, don't guess
- Find the band: what do the top sellers for the *same thing* charge (KDP genre comps, Etsy query comps, CV-service comps)? Price relative to it on purpose.
- Don't race to the bottom — too-cheap signals low quality; for digital goods marginal cost ≈ 0, so a slightly higher price at the same volume is pure profit.
- **Platform economics are real money**: KDP 70% royalty tier is **$2.99–$9.99** (a $4.99 ebook nets ~$3.35, beats a KU borrow; below $2.99 drops to 35%). Know the fee/royalty math before quoting.

## Levers beyond the number
- **Bundles raise AOV** (print sets > singles; multi-item digital goods).
- **Tiers (good-better-best)**: the $29 CV wedge → CV / CV+cover-letter / CV+LinkedIn; the middle anchors + most pick it. Only add tiers that change real scope.
- **Intro/sale pricing** lifts launch velocity (Etsy badge lifts CTR) without lowering the anchor — strikethrough, not a low base.
- Charm pricing ($X.99) + one clear price beats "contact us."

## Change it like an experiment
A price change is an ab-testing-discipline experiment: baseline volume/revenue first, one change, a
stated window, then read **profit (price × volume × margin)** not units. Don't change price + creative
at once. Live changes board-gated (marketing-ops) — propose with the comp band, margin math, expected
effect; report results to analytics-finops.

## Watch-outs
KDP 35%/70% cliff + store rules; Etsy/Stripe fees eaten before profit; never quote a price whose net (after fees) you haven't computed.
