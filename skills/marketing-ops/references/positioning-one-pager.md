# Positioning One-Pager Template

One page, board-approved before any channel work. Stored as an issue document (key `positioning`) or in the company repo; linked from every marketing issue. Rewrite it when the product changes, not per campaign.

```md
# Positioning — {{companyName}}

**Product (one sentence).** What we sell, concretely. ("Digital wall-art prints,
instant download, for home offices" — not "beautiful designs".)

**ICP — ideal customer profile.** Who buys this, described so a stranger could
spot them: situation, trigger ("just set up a home office"), where they search,
what they search for. One primary ICP; secondary ICPs listed but not targeted yet.

**Problem & alternative.** What the customer is trying to do, and what they'd
do if we didn't exist (competitor, DIY, nothing). We win against the
alternative, not in a vacuum.

**Differentiation (max 3).** Why us over the alternative — each claim verifiable
against the shipped product today. Aspirations go in a "not yet true" footnote,
never in copy.

**Proof.** What we can point at: live listings, published books, working sites,
delivered orders. If the proof list is empty, marketing's first job is creating
proof, not reach.

**Voice.** 3–5 adjectives plus one line of "we never sound like…".

**One-line pitch.** The sentence everything else compresses to. Title-tag length.
```

## Rules

- **One page means one page.** If it doesn't fit, the positioning isn't decided yet.
- Every copy deliverable in the company must be traceable to this document — same ICP, same differentiation, same voice.
- Board approval is the gate: positioning shapes everything public, so it's a board decision by definition.
- Date it and note the approver. Stale positioning (product moved on) is an escalation to the CMO.
