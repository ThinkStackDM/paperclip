# Channel Playbooks by Company Type

Operational detail behind the channel table in SKILL.md. Each playbook lists the owned-channel work to do now (free, subscription-powered) and what only unlocks later. Everything public-facing goes through the board-approval gate.

## KDP books (ThinkStack Books)

**Now — on-Amazon optimization (no ad spend):**
- **Categories.** Each book in the most specific categories it can plausibly rank in; small-pond beats big-pond. Review after each new release.
- **Keywords.** All 7 backend keyword slots filled from real search behavior (Amazon autocomplete, competitor titles). No keyword stuffing in titles — Amazon penalizes it and readers bounce.
- **Book description.** Treat as a sales page: hook line, social-proof placeholder once reviews exist, formatted with KDP's supported HTML.
- **A+ content.** Free since KDP opened it; comparison modules across the catalog as it grows — every book cross-sells the others.
- **Series & also-boughts.** Series pages and consistent branding make Amazon's own recommendation engine the distribution channel.
- KU enrollment follows the `kdp-publishing-pipeline` skill's KU-first launch decision — marketing doesn't override pipeline decisions.

**Later (board-budgeted):** Amazon ads — only after organic ranking proves the listing converts, otherwise ads pay to send traffic to a page that loses it.

## Etsy print (Dastardly Print)

**Now — listing SEO:**
- **Titles.** Front-load the buyer's search phrase; readable, not pipe-spam. One primary keyword target per listing — spread the keyword space across the catalog instead of competing with yourself.
- **Tags.** All 13, multi-word phrases, no duplicates of words already locked in attributes. Mine Etsy search autocomplete and top competitor listings.
- **Attributes & categories.** Filled completely; they are query matches, not decoration.
- **Photos.** The `etsy-listing-ops` 3-image-set flow owns production; marketing's input is which lifestyle context matches the ICP (first image wins the click).
- **Listing descriptions.** First 160 chars carry the Google snippet; lead with what+for-whom.
- **Catalog as channel.** New listings are Etsy's freshness signal — a steady publish cadence is itself SEO.

**Later (board-budgeted):** Etsy ads on proven listings only; Pinterest (drafted/approved like all social).

## Recruitment (ThinkStack Recruitment)

**Now:**
- **LinkedIn presence.** Company page + founder-voice post drafts (SocialMediaManager drafts, board posts). Cadence beats virality: market commentary in the niche, candidate-side value posts.
- **Job-board listings.** Listings are marketing — title/keyword-optimized like any listing, consistent with positioning, honest about the pipeline (per `recruitment-pipeline-ops`).
- **The $29 CV-polish wedge** is the funnel's paid entry point; every candidate touchpoint should make it discoverable without being pushy.

**Later:** BizDevSales outreach at scale (draft-only, board sends), client case studies once placements exist.

## Utility sites (ThinkStack / BrightTool Studio)

The `utility-site-shipping` skill already owns site-level SEO — the W0–W4 SEO bundle, titles/meta rewrites, GA4 wiring, CF Pages deploys. **Follow that skill; do not duplicate or contradict it.** Marketing-ops adds the layer above individual sites:

- **Portfolio keyword strategy.** Which utility to ship next is a keyword decision: search volume vs. competition per candidate tool. Feed this into the "Ship Site #N" pipeline.
- **Programmatic SEO.** Where one tool can honestly serve many query variants (per-format, per-locale, per-use-case pages), spec the page set; the site pipeline ships it.
- **Cross-linking.** Related tools under brighttoolstudio.com link to each other — internal links are free authority.
- AdSense exists here per the domain skill; that is monetization, not marketing spend.

## Media/YouTube (ThinkStack Media)

**Now — YouTube SEO (within `content-production-ops` gates, YMYL rules included):**
- **Titles.** Search-intent first, curiosity second; front-load the keyword, ≤60 chars visible.
- **Thumbnails.** Treat as the #1 conversion surface: one focal point, ≤4 words, readable at 120px. A/B alternatives drafted for board choice.
- **Descriptions & tags.** First 2 lines carry the search snippet; chapters on everything (index-able and retention-friendly).
- **Hitlist = keyword research.** The 10-video launch hitlist doubles as the keyword plan — validate search demand per video before scripting.

**Later:** Shorts repurposing from long-form, cross-promo with the portfolio's other audiences.
