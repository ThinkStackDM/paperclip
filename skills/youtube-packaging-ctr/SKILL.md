# YouTube Packaging & CTR

Thumbnail + title decide whether anyone clicks, and YouTube uses early CTR × watch-time to decide
whether to keep showing the video. Bad packaging buries a great video.

## Approval Gate

Before scripting starts, the package must be explicitly approved with:
- an approved title
- an approved thumbnail concept
- an approved hook
- an explicit package-effort target of at least 25% of the approved effort

Packaging ideation without that approval record is not a valid handoff to research or script lanes.

## Title rules
- One idea, front-loaded (payoff/curiosity in the first ~40 chars — mobile truncates the rest).
- Pick ONE of: curiosity gap / clear benefit / stakes — don't stack all three.
- Search vs browse per channel: how-to (Cashflow Compass) → include the query a viewer types;
  story (Vault Cases) → lead with intrigue. Numbers + specificity pull clicks. No clickbait the video can't pay off.

## Thumbnail rules
- Readable at 120px (mobile feed): one focal point, ≤4 words heavy-weight text, high contrast.
- Thumbnail and title must NOT repeat each other — they're a 1-2 combo (thumb = stakes/emotion, title = payoff).
- A strong object / before-after / bold number is the "face" on faceless channels. Consistent brand accent/border builds recognition.
- Generate via image-gen-ops ("16:9, single focal subject, high contrast, bold readable text in quotes"); overlay real headline text in layout — never trust the generator's small text.

## Make 3, pick on a thumb-test
1. Draft 3 title × 2 thumbnail concepts per video.
2. Thumb-test: shrink to 120px, title to ~40 chars — whichever still communicates the hook wins.
3. Compare against the top 5 results for the topic — must stand out in that grid, not blend in.
4. Do not hand off to scripting until the chosen title, thumbnail concept, and hook are explicitly approved together.

## Post-publish loop
- Pull CTR + avg-view-duration at 48h + 7d. CTR <~4% = packaging problem; good CTR + low retention = video didn't pay off the title.
- A/B by swapping the thumbnail on under-performers (free, no re-upload), logged as a marketing-ops experiment with baseline. Change the thumbnail before the title (title edits hurt early ranking).
