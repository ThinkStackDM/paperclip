# Landing Page CRO

Every page has one primary goal — design backwards from that action. (web-design-polish = trust
through looks; this = structure through to the click.)

## Structure (top→bottom)
1. **Above the fold answers 3 questions in 5s**: what is this, what do I get, what do I do next. Specific outcome headline, one-line subhead, ONE primary CTA. No carousel/menu maze.
2. **One primary CTA, repeated, same words** ("Polish my CV — $29", not "Get started"). Action + value (+ price). Demote secondary actions.
3. **Proof near the ask**: numbers, sample/before-after, trust badges (KISS: "no data leaves your browser" is a conversion asset). Real proof only.
4. **Handle the top 3 objections** inline (price, trust, effort) — usually a short FAQ.
5. **Remove friction**: fewest form fields, no forced account before value, fast load (a slow page is a CRO bug — verify with the web-design-polish Lighthouse step).

## Copy for the visitor
Lead with their outcome ("Land more interviews"), support with the mechanism. Specificity converts
("$29, 48 hours" > "affordable, fast"). One reading level down; scannable.

## Measure or it didn't happen
Define ONE conversion event + instrument it (GA4 event on CTA/submit). Know the baseline before
changing anything. Change one thing at a time (see ab-testing-discipline); claim lift vs baseline into marketing-ops + analytics-finops.

## Pre-ship checklist
One clear repeated CTA · above-fold what/value/next · proof + objections · minimal form · conversion event fires (tested live) · passes web-design-polish + Lighthouse. Live copy board-gated.
