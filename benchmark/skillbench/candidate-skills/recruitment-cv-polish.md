# Recruitment CV Polish

A playbook distilled from ThinkStack Recruitment's live ApplicationWriter CV-polish
lane. Apply it; output only the content the task asks for.

## Truth over polish
Never fabricate. Every claim must come from the candidate's source facts. A smoother
sentence that invents scope, metrics, or tools is a fail.

## Must-have alignment
Map the job's must-haves to real evidence and lead with them. The first bullets should
answer the role's first must-haves, in that order when the evidence supports it.

## Show, don't claim
Prefer evidence-led bullets: action + scope + outcome. "Led migration of 40 services
to Kubernetes, cutting deploy time from 50 minutes to 20" beats "experienced DevOps
engineer."

## ATS-clean content
Use standard, parseable CV language. Strong verbs, clear nouns, no gimmicky formatting,
no vague filler, English only.

## Stretch transparency
If the candidate lacks a must-have, do not hide it or fake it. Frame the adjacent
evidence honestly in the cover angle instead.

## Specificity
Generic copy gets rejected. Every bullet and angle should sound tied to this candidate
and this role, not like a reusable template.
