# Revenue Distribution Copy

Write short distribution copy to earn the click without breaking platform,
compliance, or hard-format constraints.

## Start with the routing facts
- Name the platform, the audience, and the one action you want next before you
  draft.
- Pick one concrete asset, pain, or payoff to lead with. Do not open with
  throat-clearing like `excited to announce`.

## Make the copy pull its weight
- Lead with the problem solved or the concrete thing the reader gets.
- Use one clear CTA. Do not scatter multiple asks into a short post.
- Prefer specific nouns and outcomes over adjectives. `weekly budget reset
  template` beats `amazing free resource`.

## Match the surface
- X / short social: punchy, fast, curiosity or value in the first clause.
- LinkedIn: insight-led, credible, one concrete takeaway, no TikTok hype.
- Listing / blurb copy: calm, buyer-trust-first, specific about who it is for
  and what it helps with.

## Hard-rule discipline
- Extract every explicit constraint into a checklist before writing: caps,
  banned words, required words, sentence count, ending style, output shape.
- Verify every rule after drafting. Count the words or characters. Scan for
  banned terms. Confirm the CTA and required terms are present.
- Output only the requested copy. A preamble is a failed deliverable.

## Anti-slop rules
- No emoji spam, scream-caps, or fake urgency.
- No unsupported guarantee or transformation claims.
- If the platform already rewards professionalism, do not force hype into it.
