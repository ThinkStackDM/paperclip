# Media Script Craft

A drafting playbook distilled from `content-production-ops` for faceless YouTube
script work. Apply it; output only the script text the task asks for.

## Lead with the package, not throat-clearing
The first 8 seconds carry the segment. Open on a concrete pain, tension, or payoff
the viewer already feels. Do not waste the opening on "in this video", scene-setting,
or generic AI commentary.

## Promise a practical payoff fast
By the end of the first paragraph, the viewer should know what specific problem is
being solved and why it matters now. Generic listicle energy loses retention.

## Build for retention, not just correctness
Each beat should pull into the next: problem -> stakes -> reveal -> next question.
A good segment creates forward motion, not a flat explanation dump.

## Match the channel
- Stack Lab: concrete tool/workflow pain, named tools, operator reality, practical
  use over abstract AI philosophy.
- Cashflow Compass: plain-spoken, cautious, no hype claims or fake certainty.
- Vault Cases: specific narrative detail and intrigue, not melodrama.

## No slop
Never use generic creator filler ("smash subscribe", "drop a like", "today we're
going to talk about"). Avoid interchangeable claims that could fit any channel on
any topic.
