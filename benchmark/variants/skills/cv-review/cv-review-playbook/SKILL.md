# Skill: CV Review (v2)

A repeatable method for reviewing a CV against a role. Apply it; output only what the task asks for.

## Step 1 — Calibrate first (this is where reviews fail)
Decide up front: strong & matched, unclear, or genuine mismatch? A clean, on-target CV ADVANCES with zero or only minor, evidence-backed notes. Never manufacture concerns to look thorough — a fabricated concern rejects a great candidate. Every concern must cite something concretely present in the CV.

## Step 2 — Integrity checks (only flag what the CV actually shows)
- **Seniority vs evidence:** does the title/level match real tenure, scope, ownership? Inflation = the claim outruns the evidence.
- **Timeline:** read all date ranges together — flag unexplained gaps AND overlapping full-time roles or an n-years claim the dates don't support.
- **Metrics:** contextless figures ('300% growth', '$5M budget', '10x team') are claims to VERIFY, not achievements. Name the specific claim and put it in the verify/clarify list (300% of what baseline? budget scope? team from what size?). Unsubstantiated numbers are a reason to CLARIFY, not to reject — and not a fatal flaw on their own.
- **Fit:** compare to the CORE required experience; name a fundamental mismatch plainly.
- **Scope + attribution discipline:** never let a rewrite outrun the source. Cap team size/scope at the maximum evidence actually supports, and do not attach team/company outcome metrics to a tool or bullet unless the source explicitly makes that causal link.
- **Workload framing:** volume growth is context unless the source says the candidate drove demand. Frame it as handling/sustaining service under growth, not as 'increased volume'.
- **Draft QA when source + rewrite are both present:** preserve all provided contact fields, keep experience entries in reverse chronological order, normalize phone/contact formatting consistently, avoid `7+` / `10+` year shorthand in candidate-voice copy, keep the strongest quantified win when compressing (or explicitly note it was dropped), and lint duplicate/repeated words before sign-off.

## Step 3 — PII discipline
DOB/age, marital/family status, photo, address, nationality are protected/irrelevant: exclude from the assessment, don't let them bias the decision, note they should be redacted. Judge only job-relevant evidence.

## Step 4 — Recommend to the evidence (be decisive, be brief)
- **advance** = strong fit, no real blockers (clean CVs land here — don't downgrade them)
- **clarify** = fixable unknowns worth an interview (unsubstantiated metrics, a single explainable gap)
- **reject** = fundamental mismatch or genuine integrity issue (irreconcilable timeline, wrong domain)

Keep concerns specific and few. Match the call to the evidence, not to a wish to seem rigorous or generous.
