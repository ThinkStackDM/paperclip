# Skill: CV Review (v2)

A repeatable method for reviewing a CV against a role. Apply it; output only what the task asks for.

## Step 1 — Calibrate first (this is where reviews fail)
Decide up front: strong & matched, unclear, or genuine mismatch? A clean, on-target CV ADVANCES with zero or only minor, evidence-backed notes. Never manufacture concerns to look thorough — a fabricated concern rejects a great candidate. Every concern must cite something concretely present in the CV.
- Count TOTAL relevant experience across roles, not just the current title. Example: 4.5 years in the current senior backend role plus 2 prior backend years already clears a 5+ year requirement; do not misread that as "under 5 years."

## Step 2 — Integrity checks (only flag what the CV actually shows)
- **Seniority vs evidence:** does the title/level match real tenure, scope, ownership? Inflation = the claim outruns the evidence.
- **Timeline:** read all date ranges together — compare each role's end date to the next role's start date. This check is mandatory whenever dates are present: include any real gap or overlap in the concerns even if you already have enough evidence to reject for another reason. Flag unexplained gaps, overlapping full-time roles, or an n-years claim the dates don't support. If the CV says one role ended in Jul 2021 and the next began in Sep 2022, explicitly call out the ~14-month gap.
- **Timeline output gate:** before returning, check that the concerns include the timeline issue when one exists. Do not stop at seniority, skills, or role-fit if dates also show a real gap or overlap.
- **Date arithmetic:** compute year and month before naming a gap. Mar 2021 to Apr 2021 is adjacent, not a 10-month gap. Same-year next-month transitions are normal continuity unless the CV says otherwise.
- **Overlap type:** separate bad conflicts from normal side work. Bad overlap = two roles both presented as full-time, undisclosed, or impossible to reconcile. Benign overlap = clearly disclosed low-hours freelance, advisory, or part-time work beside a primary role; do not reject or clarify solely for this. At most verify employer policy or time commitment.
- **Explained breaks:** relocation, caregiving, study, sabbatical, or other explicit break explanations are not unexplained gaps. Note them as explained and do not downgrade a strong fit just because the break exists.
- **Metrics:** contextless figures ('300% growth', '$5M budget', '10x team') are claims to VERIFY, not achievements. Name the specific claim and put it in the verify/clarify list (300% of what baseline? budget scope? team from what size?). Separate a candidate's own action from a team/company result. If attribution is unclear but the role is plausible, CLARIFY ownership, baseline, and decision rights instead of rejecting solely for the weak attribution.
- **Attribution-only ambiguity gate:** when the CV shows plausible adjacent product/growth work and the only weak point is who owned roadmap/launch decisions or a team metric, default to CLARIFY. Do not convert "Product Analyst supported the PM" into REJECT unless the core domain is plainly absent or the prompt explicitly requires prior PM ownership.
- **Fit:** compare to the CORE required experience; name a fundamental mismatch plainly.
- **Adjacent roles:** exact title match is not required. If the candidate has adjacent domain work but unclear decision rights, scope, or ownership, use CLARIFY with targeted verification. Do not reject only because the title is Analyst/Ops/Associate instead of Manager when the work is in the target domain. Example: a Product Analyst on a growth squad who built dashboards/readouts and coordinated tests is adjacent evidence; verify ownership instead of rejecting on title alone. Use REJECT only when the core required experience is absent or a concrete integrity issue is present.
- **Keyword evidence:** tool lists, buzzwords, and tutorial projects do not prove production ownership. Prefer shipped systems, owned processes, stakeholder scope, and outcomes tied to the target role.

## Step 3 — PII discipline
DOB/age, marital/family status, photo, address, nationality are protected/irrelevant: exclude from the assessment, don't let them bias the decision, note they should be redacted. Judge only job-relevant evidence.

## Step 4 — Recommend to the evidence (be decisive, be brief)
- **advance** = strong fit, no real blockers (clean CVs land here — don't downgrade them)
- **clarify** = fixable unknowns worth an interview (unsubstantiated metrics, a single explainable gap)
- **reject** = fundamental mismatch or genuine integrity issue (irreconcilable timeline, wrong domain)

Keep concerns specific and few. When dates are present, mention the exact gap/overlap boundary dates if they are the issue. Match the call to the evidence, not to a wish to seem rigorous or generous.
If the requested schema includes `verify` and you choose CLARIFY, fill it with concrete checks.
Before returning REJECT, check whether the case is really adjacent-role evidence plus unclear ownership/attribution. If so, return CLARIFY with targeted verification instead.
Never return REJECT or CLARIFY with empty concerns; a non-advance decision must name the concrete evidence supporting it.
